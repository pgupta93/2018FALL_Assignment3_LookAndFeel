Nail Polish
  • What is Nail Polish? Nail polish (also known as nail varnish) is a lacquer that can be applied to the human fingernail or toenails to decorate and protect the nail plates. The formulation has been revised repeatedly to enhance its decorative effects, and to suppress cracking or flaking. Nail polish consists of a mix of an organic polymer and several other components, depending on the brand. People use this liquid to create both simple and complicated designs with bright colors, charms, or other.
  • How to use Nail Polish? Place the nail polish brush close to the cuticle area and push it up to get as close to the cuticle line as you can. Ideally leaving a gap which is only the width of a hair. And then drag the brush towards the free edge. Spread the base coat to the sides.
    Allow the polish to dry and then apply the second coat in the same way but with more polish and less pressure in the brush, so that the brush wonâ€™t dig into the first coat.

Nail Polish Remover
  • What is Nail Polish Remover? It is the chemical used to remove nail polish.

    Generally nail polish remover is made of acetone, which is a solvent that dissolves the film of the nail polish. Because of acetone's strength as a solvent, it shouldn't be used on your fake nails. The solvent in the polish remover will weaken your extensions and cause them to separate from your natural nail. One of the reasons non-acetone nail polish removers were created was to be used on nail extensions.

    Non-acetone polish removers contain ethyl acetate or methyl ethyl keytone as their active ingredient and are less effective for removing nail polish than acetone (slower removal).
  • How to use Nail Polish Remover? Open the lid of the nail polish remover and firmly place the cotton round over the opening & saturate the cotton round with nail polish remover.

    Place the saturated cotton round over your fingernail and apply pressure. Rub the cotton round back and forth, up and down across the nail.

    Use as many fresh remover-soaked cotton pads as you need. Repeat the procedure with each fingernail.

    Rub a fresh cotton round with nail polish remover over each nail to remove any excess residue or polish that might have been left behind.

    Wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water & pat your hands dry.
